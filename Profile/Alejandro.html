Alejan​dro Sánchez-Pedreño Jiménez

Email: a.sanchez-pedrenojimenez@surrey.ac.uk

Room: 06AW01

Alejandro is a graduate student at the QB-DTC at the University of Surrey, where he studies the “Room Temperature Coherent Interactions Between Fluorescent Proteins.” He received his B.Sc. degree in Biotechnology, University of Surrey, UK. Alejandro has a long-standing interest in fluorescent proteins and its wide applications as biosensors. He believes that fluorescent proteins, quantum physics theories and molecular biology techniques may come together to develop next-generation quantum biosensors. 

His research is focused on performing mutagenic studies to alter the structural, physical and optical properties of the fluorescent proteins for ultrafast optical spectroscopy characterisation. He anticipates that the development of quantum biosensors could have extensive potential applications including quantum computers, nano-electronics and single photon emitters at room temperature. 

Primary supervisor: Dr Youngchan Kim / Co-supervisor: Prof Ravi Silva
